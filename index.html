About JT

I'd rather make programs with people than push them on people

You don't have to persuade a computer to act a certain way, it either will or it won't.

I've always liked technology, but until recently I was focused on selling, not developing it. After entering the workforce, I knew sales wasn't my passion, but it was the direction in which I was most familiar and qualified. Coding was always interesting to me, so when Covid-19 put many things on hold, I decided to dive in by taking a Web Development bootcamp at Eleven Fifty, which is when my interest turned into a passion.

Previously I worked at a large technology reseller, where I developed sales-level relationships with leading software brands like Microsoft, AWS, Zoom, Adobe, and VMware. I knew enough about each to get on the phone and pitch meetings, but always had a desire to work with the technical aspects that miraculously allow their programs (and all programs) to do what they do.

I am humbled and excited to know I have only scratched the surface of the development world. While the "soft skills" I developed in the sales are less relevant here, I still feel they will help me while collaborating with teammates or communicating to a manager or client. I feel confidently that my newfound passion for coding, along with my desire to solve problems with people will allow me to succeed in this path.
